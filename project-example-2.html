Anonymized example project

Giving docent teams a shared source of truth.

DataSail built a cross-platform mobile app that let docent and volunteer teams update animal visibility status in realtime, so any team member could answer visitor questions from the latest information.

Mobile workflow app Realtime status updates Visitor experience support

Problem statement

Could every docent know what was visible right now?

Visitors often ask whether animals are currently visible in an exhibit. The operating challenge was keeping that information current across multiple docents without relying on word of mouth, radio chatter, or one person having the latest update.

Solution summary

Build a simple field app around the real workflow.

DataSail built an Android and iOS app with grouped exhibit areas, green/gray stoplight status, last-updated timestamps, tap-to-cycle updates, and realtime cloud synchronization through Firebase.

Value realized

Turn scattered updates into visitor-ready answers.

The app gave docent teams a shared, lightweight source of truth. Any docent could check or update status from the floor and answer visitor questions based on the most recent team input.

What was delivered

A practical mobile system, not a spreadsheet workaround.

The app was designed around speed and clarity: large tap targets, simple status colors, grouped areas, and timestamps that made it obvious when information was fresh.

01

Grouped exhibit views

Animals were organized by area so docents could scan the part of the venue they were working.

02

Tap-to-update status

Rows could be updated with a simple interaction, using green and gray status indicators for fast interpretation.

03

Realtime synchronization

Firebase kept the shared list current across devices, with anonymous authentication and server timestamps.

04

Cross-platform delivery

The same Flutter codebase supported Android and iOS delivery without building two separate apps from scratch.

Result

A small app solved a real coordination problem.

This project turned informal status-sharing into a durable operating tool. The value was not a flashy interface; it was making the right answer available at the moment a visitor asked for it.

Users Docents

Platform iOS + Android

Delivered as a practical mobile app for the devices teams already carried.
